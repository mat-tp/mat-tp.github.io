Research & current work

Image Inpainting via Deep Reinforcement Learning

Instead of relying solely on CNNs or Encoder-Decoder networks, I explored using a Reinforcement Learning agent for image reconstruction tasks. Training an RL agent to optimality is computationally demanding, and the results are to be explored in the future, with the new approach that is structural architecturally novel and Promising.

↗ github.com/mat-tp/deep_rl_agent_Image_restoration

Video Classification under Different Camera Distortions In progress

Focusing on pure ML techniques, including KNN, SVM, and Decision Trees to classify video content across different distortion types: Pristine, Exposure, Blur, and Exposure-Blur combined into classes based on the surveillance video dataset.

↗ github.com/mat-tp/suspicious-activity-detection

South African Language Predictability Comparisons In progress

An NLP study examining how predictable South African languages are relative to each other, motivated by the scarcity of training data for these languages and a goal of supporting NLP system development for them. Currently in my research I have developed language generation model for 4 South African languages, technically 2 (isiZule and Sepedi) against English and Afrikaans, evaluating using Entropy and Perplexity metrics to determine which language is more predictable and easier to model. The research is incomplete as not all possible South African language dataset is not used and also as there are missing languages that are not included in the research.

↗ github.com/mat-tp/SA-Language-Predictability
